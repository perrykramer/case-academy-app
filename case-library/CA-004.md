# Catch Me or I Go, HuDisney

**Source:** UVA Darden School of Business, 2024-2025 Casebook  
**Type:** M&A · **Industry:** Media & Entertainment · **Difficulty:** 3/5  
**Format:** Candidate-led · **Stretch area:** Exhibit reading + multi-step subscriber math

---

## Prompt
*Read this to the candidate.*

Your client is Disney, a global leader in entertainment with a diverse portfolio of media networks, including Disney+ and Hulu. Disney+ is the flagship streaming service and focuses on family content, while Hulu offers a wider range of TV shows and movies. As the streaming market grows more competitive and consumers prefer consolidated services — multiple networks bundled into one platform — Disney is considering merging Hulu into Disney+ to streamline its offerings. You're tasked with analyzing this integration's viability and strategizing its implementation.

---

## Clarifying Questions
*A strong candidate would ask 2-3 of the following before structuring their approach:*

**Q:** What is Disney hoping to achieve by merging the platforms?  
**A:** Disney hopes to increase revenues from its streaming channel.

**Q:** What are the differences between the platforms' content, size, or genre?  
**A:** Disney+ focuses on family-friendly content, including Pixar, Star Wars, and Marvel titles. Hulu offers a broader range of content from network TV (e.g., ABC), movies, and original programming.

**Q:** What geographical markets do Disney+ and Hulu serve?  
**A:** Both services have subscribers across the globe, and the two platforms serve similar geographic markets.

This is an M&A case with both quantitative (revenue synergies, break-even economics) and qualitative (organizational integration, brand fit) components. The candidate should structure around four pillars: the external streaming market, the standalone economics of the deal, Disney's internal capability to execute the integration, and the marketing/positioning of the combined platform.

## Question 1: Reading the streaming market
*(Ask after the candidate has made initial comments on Exhibit 1.)*
> *How do you think the integration of Hulu into the Disney+ platform could alter Disney's position in the market?*

### Exhibit A: Streaming Service Market Share
![Exhibit A: Streaming Service Market Share](/case-library/CA-004/exhibit_a.png)
**Note:** Share of total U.S. streaming subscription revenue by platform.

<div class="case-calculation">

| Platform | Market share |
|---|---|
| Netflix | 40% |
| Disney+ | 30% |
| Amazon Prime | 15% |
| Hulu | 10% |
| Others (Apple TV, Max, Peacock, etc.) | 5% |
| **Total** | **100%** |

</div>

**Observations candidates should surface:**
- **Competition:** three players — Netflix, Disney+, Amazon Prime — make up 85% of the market; Netflix leads outright.
- **Combined position:** Disney+ and Hulu together already hold 40% of streaming revenue, equal to Netflix. If the integration captures incremental revenue, Disney becomes the outright leader.
- **Market structure:** the "Others" tail is just 5% — a fragmented long tail, leaving little room for competitors to close the gap through their own consolidation.

**Sample answer:** Combining Hulu into Disney+ pushes Disney's combined share to parity with Netflix (40% each) and materially ahead of Amazon Prime (15%). Because the next tier of competitors is fragmented (5% "Others"), there's little near-term threat of another player closing the gap through M&A of its own — so the real question becomes whether the combined platform converts share into higher revenue per user, not whether it can defend share.

## Question 2: Sizing the bundled subscription
> *If the integration is successful, Disney+ plans to introduce a bundled subscription that offers both Disney+ and Hulu content at a discount versus subscribing separately. Calculate the expected annual revenue from the new bundle and compare it to today's separate-subscription revenue.*

**Data provided (ask for it before calculating):**
- Current subscribers: Disney+ 100M; Hulu 35M; 15M subscribe to both
- Current pricing: Disney+ $7/month; Hulu $11/month
- Bundled pricing: $15/month for both services
- Adoption: 20% of Disney+-only subscribers and 25% of Hulu-only subscribers switch to the bundle in year one

### Math

**Step 1: Current revenue baseline**

<div class="case-calculation">

| Service | Subscribers | Price/mo | Annual revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Disney+ | 100M | $7 | $8.40B |
| Hulu | 35M | $11 | $4.62B |
| **Total (today)** | | | **$13.02B** |

</div>

**Step 2: Subscriber migration to the bundle**

<div class="case-calculation">

**Disney+-only subscribers switching** = (100M − 15M overlap) × 20% = 85M × 20% = **17M**

**Hulu-only subscribers switching** = (35M − 15M overlap) × 25% = 20M × 25% = **5M**

**Total bundle subscribers** = 17M + 5M + 15M (existing overlap) = **37M**

</div>

**Sanity check:** the bundle figure should land between the 15M who already double-subscribe and the smaller standalone base (35M) — 37M is just outside that range because it includes the pre-existing overlap plus new switchers, which is expected. A number below 15M or above 100M signals a subscriber-count error.

**Step 3: Projected revenue**

<div class="case-calculation">

| Segment | Subscribers | Price/mo | Annual revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bundle (both services) | 37M | $15 | $6.66B |
| Disney+ only (remaining) | 68M | $7 | $5.712B |
| Hulu only (remaining) | 15M | $11 | $1.98B |
| **Total (projected)** | | | **$14.35B** |

</div>

**Answer:** The bundle grows annual streaming revenue from $13.02B to roughly $14.35B — a gain of about **$1.33B (+10%)**.

## Question 3: Marketing the combined platform
> *If the network integration goes through, what marketing strategies should Disney deploy to maximize both subscriber retention and growth?*

A strong candidate structures brainstorming into categories and provides at least 4 ideas (top candidates provide 7-8):

| Category | Ideas |
|---|---|
| Digital advertising | SEM campaigns; SEO optimization; targeted social media campaigns; personalized ads |
| Content marketing | Exclusive releases and previews; influencer partnerships across social platforms |
| Customer engagement | Subscription loyalty rewards; flexible family plans; community building through reviews; interactive live events |

<div style="margin-top: 1.5rem;"></div>

**Best practice:** the strongest answers reference leveraging the diversity of combined content, using multiple online platforms for advertising, and fostering a deeper connection between the brand and its customers — rather than listing generic marketing tactics.

## Recommendation
> *To conclude, what's your recommendation?*

**Recommendation:** The case supports either direction — the source frames both a "proceed" and a "hold" path as defensible. The stronger answer path is to **proceed** with the integration, paired with the bundled subscription launch, provided Disney manages the rollout in phases.

**Supporting reasoning:**
1. Combined Disney+/Hulu share already matches Netflix (40% each) — the deal converts a share tie into outright leadership.
2. The bundle model expands the addressable base without cannibalizing full price — projected revenue rises ~$1.33B (+10%) even under conservative adoption assumptions (20% / 25%).
3. Content strengths are complementary rather than overlapping — Disney+'s family catalog and Hulu's broader/adult catalog together should improve retention.

**Risks / considerations:**
- **Subscriber churn:** existing subscribers may react negatively to changes in interface, content, or subscription model.
- **Brand dilution:** Hulu's more mature content sitting inside the Disney-branded ecosystem could weaken family-brand positioning — or force content restrictions that erode Hulu's own value proposition, eating into the projected revenue gain.

**Next steps:**
- Build a phased integration plan with clearly communicated rollout stages to limit churn shock.
- Market-test the bundle in a subset of regions before a full launch to validate the 20%/25% adoption assumptions.

## Interviewer Notes

**Great candidates will:**
- Immediately net out the exhibit to "combined share = Netflix's share" rather than just reading off percentages
- Track the subscriber overlap carefully — the easy error is double-counting, or forgetting to subtract the 15M who already subscribe to both before applying the switch rates
- Sanity-check the 37M bundle figure against the 100M/35M standalone bases
- Argue both the FOR and AGAINST case and still land on a clear recommendation, rather than staying neutral
- Structure the marketing brainstorm into distinct categories rather than a flat list

**Common pitfalls:**
- Forgetting to subtract the 15M double-subscribers before applying the 20%/25% switch rates (this inflates the bundle count)
- Stating $14.35B as a big jump without also framing it in percentage terms (~+10%) for the interviewer
- Missing the brand-dilution risk (adult Hulu content inside the family-oriented Disney+ ecosystem) when arguing FOR the deal
- A flat, uncategorized marketing brainstorm list

**Signal to push on:** Ask, *"What if adoption comes in at half your assumed rate — does the recommendation change?"* Strong candidates recognize the bundle still grows revenue (fewer switchers means less discount-driven cannibalization) and can re-run the math directionally without a calculator.

**Optional opener (if time allows):** a quick behavioral question before starting the case — *"What industry fascinates you? Please share something current happening in that industry."*
